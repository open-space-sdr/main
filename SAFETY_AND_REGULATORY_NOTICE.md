These files do not by themselves constitute a certified radio product, approved transmitter, approved antenna system, or authorization to transmit radio-frequency energy in any jurisdiction. Any person or entity that builds, modifies, sells, installs, connects, powers, transmits with, imports, exports, or operates hardware based on these files is responsible for determining and complying with all applicable laws, regulations, licenses, equipment-authorization requirements, RF-exposure limits, electrical-safety requirements, export/import rules, and site-specific safety obligations.

## High-power RF and phased-array risks

High-power RF systems, amplifiers, directional antennas, and phased arrays can create hazardous RF exposure levels, strong near fields, high effective radiated power, interference to other spectrum users, thermal hazards, electrical hazards, fire hazards, and equipment damage.

Do not connect the released antenna designs to transmitters, RF power amplifiers, phased-array transmit systems, automated beamforming systems, or high-gain antenna configurations unless you are qualified to evaluate the resulting RF exposure, conducted and radiated emissions, antenna gain, EIRP, harmonic and spurious emissions, duty cycle, thermal behavior, grounding, lightning protection, and interference risk.

## User responsibility

Users are solely responsible for lawful operation. This includes, without limitation:

- operating only in authorized frequency bands;
- using appropriate licenses, exemptions, or equipment authorizations;
- observing applicable power, antenna-gain, and EIRP limits;
- avoiding harmful interference;
- maintaining safe RF-exposure separation distances;
- evaluating near-field and far-field exposure hazards;
- validating spurious and harmonic emissions;
- using appropriate filters, attenuators, dummy loads, shielding, and test equipment;
- preventing overheating, arcing, fire risk, or electrical shock;
- ceasing operation immediately if harmful interference, unsafe exposure, overheating, arcing, fire risk, or other unsafe conditions occur.
